Movie Database Website Development

Background

React is a popular JavaScript library for building user interfaces. It is used by many companies and developers around the world to create efficient and scalable web applications.

There are several reasons why people use React for web development. One of the main reasons is that React allows developers to create reusable components, which can make it easier to build complex and scalable user interfaces. Additionally, React uses a virtual DOM, which can improve application performance by reducing the amount of time it takes to update the view when the state of the application changes. Finally, React has a strong ecosystem, with a large community of developers and a wide range of third-party libraries and tools that can be used to extend its capabilities.

In this project, we will be using React to develop a movie database website that allows users to search for and view information about their favorite movies. The website will feature a clean and intuitive design, and will be powered by a powerful interactive feature that will enable users to quickly and easily find the movie they are looking for. We believe that by using React, we will be able to create a website that is fast, user-friendly, and capable of handling a large amount of data. We are excited to get started and are looking forward to creating a valuable resource for movie fans everywhere.

Research

Proto Persona

Meet Jane, a 35-year-old film enthusiast from Los Angeles. Jane has been a movie fan since she was a child and loves nothing more than curling up on the couch with a bowl of popcorn and a good film.

She is an avid user of the movie database websites and uses them regularly to find out more about her favorite movies and discover new films to watch. She is always on the lookout for the latest releases and enjoys reading reviews and ratings to help her decide what to watch. In her free time, Jane also enjoys attending film festivals and is a member of several online movie communities.

She is a social media addict and loves sharing her thoughts on movies with her friends and followers.

Storyboard

Scene 1:

Jane is sitting on her couch, scrolling through the IMDb app on her phone. She is trying to decide what to watch on TV tonight.

Scene 2:

Jane finds a movie that she has been wanting to see and taps on its title to view more information. She reads the synopsis, reviews, and ratings to help her make a decision.

Scene 3:

Jane decides to watch the movie and adds it to her watchlist. She then posts a message on her social media account to let her friends know what she will be watching tonight.

Scene 4:

After watching the movie, Jane goes back to IMDb to rate it and leave a review. She also checks out the cast and crew information to learn more about the people behind the film.

Scene 5:

Jane continues to use IMDb to discover new movies and to keep track of her watchlist. She also uses the app to participate in online discussions with other movie fans and to share her thoughts on the latest releases.

Design

Filters

It provides users with the ability to filter the movies by genre or by rating so that they can select a movie that they're interested to watch.

Filtering by genre can help users to find the movies they want by allowing them to narrow down their search to a specific category of films.

Filtering by rating can help users to find the movies they want by allowing them to narrow down their search to films that have received a certain level of critical acclaim.

Sort

The user can sort the movie database by score or by year release.

Sorting by movie score can help users to find the movies they want by allowing them to prioritize films that have received a high score from critics or other users.

Sorting by movie release year can help users to find the movies they want by allowing them to arrange the search results based on when the films were released.

Add to My Favorite

The user can add the movie they like to "my favorite" list so they can later refer to find they movies they are interested in.

They can do so by selecting the number they want to add and then hit submit button to down below to make the change.

The adjusting number function was design to allow user to track how my times they have watched the movie.

My Favorite List

Having a "My Favorite List" function on a movie database website can improve the user experience in several ways. First, it can allow users to easily save and organize the movies they are interested in, making it easier for them to keep track of which films they want to watch and to access this information quickly in the future. This can be especially helpful for users who have a large number of favorite movies and want to be able to easily access and manage this information.

Second, a "My Favorite List" function can provide users with personalized recommendations based on their favorite movies. For example, the website can use the information in a user's favorite list to suggest similar films that they might be interested in. This can help users to discover new movies that they might not have considered otherwise, and can make their browsing experience on the website more enjoyable and effective.

Final Prototype

Usability Principles Considered

The usability principle I considered here was to make the sorting and filtering as simple to understand and use as possible. I designed the website so that the user can easily find what genre they want to use to filter the movie list and which property they want to sort the movie by so that they can easily find the most ideal movie. The sorting and filtering bar does not scroll with the movie list, making it easier to use. The user can select the number of movie tickets to purchase and then click submit to add the film to their favorites. They can also change the quantity and remove the movie.

Organization of Components

The sorting by year or by score is in the sorting block and remains on the left bar, as do the filters by genre and rating, which remain as the user scrolls down the movie list. The middle of the movie card list's body contains a movie image, movie information, and an add-to-favorites feature. On the right side of each movie card, there is a button that allows you to change the quantity and add it to your favorites list. By clicking on the My favorites section at the top right of the page, the user can see what is in their favorite list.

How Data is Passed Down Through Components

I have ListComponent representing the movie card list, and OperationComponent representing the sorting and filter operations when selected on the left navbar. The ScoreList component calculates the total score added to the favorite.

The props pass down from the app to the sub-component using a function like setSort, setGenre. The function is implemented in the sub-component with useEffect.

How the User Triggers State Changes

Use useState to track the state of the sorting method, genre for the filter, score for filter, and list of favorite movies.

Use useEffect to update the filtered array list of movies after the user checked the check box on the left operation bar. After the user checked the checkbox for a specific genre or rating to filter the list, the array will render the changes.
